# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Bank officers, accounting staff, and financial administrators at Indonesian banks (BNI, BRI, Bank Mandiri) who regularly receive monthly bank statements in PDF format and need to reconcile or process them in Excel. They work at a desk, on a local or company laptop, under time pressure to complete reconciliation tasks. Most are moderately tech-literate — comfortable with Excel, not with code.

Admin users (branch/regional supervisors or IT admins) who manage which staff members have access and for how long (including trial accounts with expiry).

## Product Purpose

Convert PDF rekening koran (bank statements) into structured Excel files with a single upload. Eliminates manual data entry and copy-paste errors during monthly reconciliation. Access is controlled — authenticated tool, not public — so only authorized users at the organization can use it.

## Brand Personality

Professional, reliable, frictionless. The tool should feel like bank-grade software: trustworthy and precise, not flashy. Three words: **Trustworthy · Efficient · Clean**.

## Anti-references

- Overly playful SaaS landing-page aesthetics (gradient text, hero metrics, card grids)
- Consumer apps (too casual for a financial tool)
- Legacy government/enterprise UI (too heavy and dated — users already deal with that elsewhere)

## Design Principles

1. **The tool disappears into the task.** A user uploading a PDF statement is focused on their reconciliation work, not the UI. Every chrome element that isn't helping them should be invisible.
2. **Authority without intimidation.** The interface should feel bank-grade — authoritative, clean — but not bureaucratic. Clear hierarchy, no confusion about what to do next.
3. **Trust is earned by reliability.** Consistent component vocabulary, predictable states, meaningful feedback on success and error. Never leave the user guessing whether something worked.
4. **Access control is a first-class concern.** The admin and auth surfaces are not afterthoughts. They carry the same quality bar as the converter itself.
5. **Indonesian context, universal craft.** Copy is in Bahasa Indonesia; the visual quality should match international product standards.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Users are on standard corporate laptops in office lighting — no known specific accessibility requirements beyond baseline. Keyboard navigation required for all interactive elements. No specific reduced-motion policy stated; honor the OS preference.
